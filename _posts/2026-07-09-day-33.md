what_i_learned: |
  Today marked the beginning of the technical implementation of our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework. I started implementing the ideas we have been discussing over the past several weeks to address modality dominance in multimodal learning. The goal of this framework is to ensure that more informative biological modalities contribute more strongly during prediction while reducing the influence of modalities that provide weaker predictive signals.

  To better understand the behavior of each modality, I conducted several experiments using different fusion strategies. I evaluated both early fusion and late fusion approaches and compared their performance using AUROC scores. The results continued to support what we had observed previously: the genotype modality consistently produced weaker predictive performance than the gene expression and DNA methylation modalities. Running single-modality evaluations further confirmed that genotype contributed the least predictive signal, reinforcing our motivation for developing a signal-gated fusion approach.

  I also implemented baseline experiments using XGBoost to compare its performance with our existing models. In addition, I began developing an inner-fold validation procedure that will allow us to estimate the predictive strength of each modality during training. These modality-specific scores will later be used to assign adaptive weights before evaluating the model on the outer validation folds. Learning about nested evaluation strategies helped me better understand how robust machine learning experiments are designed while avoiding information leakage between training and evaluation.

  Overall, today's work represented an important milestone in our project because it marked the transition from validating our research hypothesis to implementing the framework designed to address the problem we identified.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The primary challenge was carefully designing the weighting strategy so that modality importance is learned only from the training data and can be evaluated fairly without introducing data leakage.

reflection: |
  Today was one of the most rewarding days of the project so far because I began implementing the core idea behind our proposed framework. It was exciting to see the experimental results support our hypothesis that not all modalities contribute equally to prediction. Seeing the genotype layer consistently produce weaker predictive performance reinforced the motivation behind SGOF and gave me confidence that we are addressing a meaningful problem. I am looking forward to continuing the implementation and evaluating whether our adaptive weighting strategy can improve the robustness and predictive performance of our multimodal model.
