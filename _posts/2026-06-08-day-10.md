what_i_learned: |
 Today was heavily focused on the practical side of our research project as we continued working through the early stages of our methodology. A significant portion of the day was dedicated to data exploration and data cleaning. We began by loading and examining our dataset to better understand its structure, the available variables, and the types of information it contains. Through this process, we started identifying potential data quality issues, inconsistencies, and areas that may require preprocessing before the data can be used effectively in later stages of the project.

  We also spent time exploring the dataset in greater depth by examining different features and discussing which variables may be most relevant to our research objectives. This initial feature exploration and selection process helped us gain a better understanding of the information available to us and how it may contribute to answering our research questions. As we analyzed the data, we began thinking critically about which features would be most useful for our planned simulations and evaluations.

  In addition to the technical work, our team spent time revisiting and refining our methodology. Since we have now started implementing the first phase of our project, it was important for us to carefully review each step of the methodology and understand how the different components connect together. Walking through the methodology in detail helped me better understand the overall workflow of the project, the purpose behind each stage, and how our current work fits into the larger research goals. Overall, today's activities helped transform many of the ideas from our design document into a more concrete and actionable plan.

  One challenge I encountered today was gaining familiarity with the structure and complexity of the dataset. Since there are many variables and potential features to consider, it can be difficult to determine which pieces of information will ultimately be the most relevant for our research objectives. However, continued exploration, team discussions, and a deeper understanding of the methodology are helping us narrow our focus and make more informed decisions.
reflection: |
  Today's work gave me a much deeper appreciation for the importance of data preparation in the research process. Before any meaningful analysis or modeling can take place, it is essential to thoroughly understand the dataset, identify potential issues, and determine which features are most relevant to the problem being studied. I realized that data cleaning and exploration are not simply preliminary tasks, but foundational steps that directly impact the quality and reliability of future results.

  I also found it valuable to revisit our methodology now that we have begun actively working through it. Reading a methodology on paper is very different from implementing it in practice. As we discussed each step in greater detail, I developed a clearer understanding of how the various components of our project connect and contribute to our overall research goals. Collaborating with my teammates throughout this process was particularly helpful because it allowed us to share ideas, ask questions, and build a stronger collective understanding of the work ahead. Overall, I ended the day with a better understanding of both our data and the direction of our project, and I feel more prepared for the next stages of the research process.
