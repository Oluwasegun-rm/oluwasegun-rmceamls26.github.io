what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on understanding the technical design of the next major component of our research project, Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF). Our proposed framework is designed to improve the robustness and generalization of our graph neural network while addressing one of the key challenges in multimodal learning: modality dominance. We also discussed how the framework can remain effective even when one of the input modalities contains little useful information or becomes an uninformative layer.

  A large portion of the day was spent meeting with my graduate mentor to better understand how SGOF will be implemented within our model. We discussed how the different omics modalities will interact, how the signal gating mechanism is intended to influence the learning process, and why balancing the contribution of each modality is important for improving overall model performance. These conversations helped me connect the theoretical concepts I have been reading about with the practical implementation that we will soon begin developing.

  We also discussed how we plan to evaluate the effectiveness of our proposed framework. Rather than simply building the model, we will compare its performance against baseline approaches using evaluation metrics to determine whether SGOF provides measurable improvements in predictive performance and robustness. Since I have already completed much of the data preprocessing work, today's focus was primarily on understanding the implementation strategy and the evaluation plan before moving into the coding phase.

  Overall, today's discussions gave me a much clearer picture of how the different pieces of our project fit together and prepared me for the implementation work that will begin after further discussions with our faculty mentor.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The main task was developing a solid understanding of the proposed SGOF framework before implementation. My next step is to meet with our faculty mentor to further refine the design and confirm our implementation approach.

reflection: |
  Today reinforced the importance of fully understanding a model before attempting to implement it. Spending time discussing the architecture, expected behavior, and evaluation strategy with my graduate mentor gave me much more confidence in the direction of our project. I also appreciated seeing how research involves careful planning, validation, and comparison—not simply building a model, but demonstrating why a proposed method performs better than existing approaches.
