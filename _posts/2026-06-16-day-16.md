what_i_learned: |
  Today's work focused heavily on dataset exploration, data loading, and understanding the types of data that may be useful for our research project. A significant portion of the day was spent searching for datasets related to Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), particularly datasets that contain information about genes and their potential relationships to the condition. As part of this process, I explored multiple data sources and reviewed related research papers to identify datasets that have been used in previous studies. This helped me better understand how researchers select datasets and how existing literature can guide the search for relevant data.

  I also spent time loading and examining several datasets using Python and Google Colab. By exploring the structure of these datasets, reviewing available features, and analyzing the types of information they contained, I was able to evaluate whether they would be suitable for our project's goals. This process reinforced the importance of understanding a dataset before attempting any analysis or modeling.

  In addition, I continued developing my data cleaning and preprocessing skills. Working directly with datasets gave me more experience handling different file formats, inspecting data quality, and thinking critically about how data can be prepared for future analysis. Overall, today's activities helped me gain a deeper appreciation for the amount of work that goes into finding, evaluating, and preparing data for research projects.

blockers: |
  One challenge I encountered today involved working with a `.tar` file format, which I had not used before. Initially, I experienced difficulty determining how to properly extract and load the contents into Python for analysis. This slowed down my workflow for a period of time, but after conducting some research and experimenting with different approaches, I was able to understand the format and successfully access the data. The experience reinforced the importance of persistence and problem-solving when working with unfamiliar data sources.
reflection: |
  Today showed me that finding the right dataset can be just as important as the analysis itself. While it can be tempting to jump directly into modeling or experimentation, I am beginning to understand that the quality, relevance, and structure of the data ultimately determine what kinds of conclusions can be drawn from a project. Exploring datasets related to Major Depressive Disorder and reviewing how other researchers approached similar problems helped me better appreciate the importance of connecting research questions with appropriate data sources.

  I also found myself becoming more comfortable with independently investigating unfamiliar concepts and technical challenges. As I worked through different datasets and file formats, I realized that many research tasks involve learning things that you have never encountered before. Rather than viewing this as a setback, I am starting to see it as a normal part of the learning process. Overall, today's work strengthened both my technical skills and my confidence in navigating new problems, and I feel that I am gradually becoming more comfortable with the exploratory nature of research.
