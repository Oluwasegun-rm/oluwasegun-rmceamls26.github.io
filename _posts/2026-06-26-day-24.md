what_i_learned: |
  Today was primarily focused on strengthening our preparation for the Mid-Summer Research Symposium. My team spent time reviewing our presentation as a whole, making revisions to both the slides and our delivery to ensure that our research could be presented in a clear, organized, and engaging way. We worked on improving the transitions between sections, refining how we explained our methodology and results, and making sure each team member could confidently present their assigned portion.

  As part of our preparation, we delivered several practice presentations and received constructive feedback from faculty members, graduate mentors, and fellow researchers. These practice sessions helped us identify areas where our explanations could be more concise, where visuals could better support our message, and where additional clarification would improve the audience's understanding. The experience emphasized the value of refining both the technical content and the way it is communicated.

  I also had the opportunity to attend other teams' practice presentations and observe the progress they have made on their respective research projects. Listening to their presentations and the feedback they received exposed me to a variety of research topics, presentation techniques, and communication styles. Overall, today's activities helped me become more comfortable presenting technical material and better prepared me for next week's symposium.

reflection: |
  Preparing for the symposium reminded me that presenting research requires a different skill set than conducting the research itself. It challenged me to think about how to explain complex ideas clearly while remaining accurate and confident. Receiving feedback and observing other presentations gave me practical ideas that I can continue applying to improve both my communication skills and my understanding of our project.
