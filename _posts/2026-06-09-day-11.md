what_i_learned: |
  Today’s activities focused on strengthening our research writing skills and continuing to refine our understanding of our project. During our literature review session, we learned about the importance of transitional words and phrases in academic writing. We discussed how these transitions help improve the flow of ideas by connecting sentences and paragraphs in a logical and organized way. Different types of transitional phrases can be used to show similarities, highlight contrasts, draw conclusions, introduce supporting evidence, emphasize important points, and identify research gaps within existing literature. Learning how to use these transitions effectively helped me better understand how researchers build coherent arguments and communicate complex ideas clearly.
  In addition to the writing session, our team spent time discussing our dataset and how it can be used to support our research objectives. We examined the information available within the dataset and considered how different variables and features may contribute to our project. These discussions helped us think more critically about how to frame our research and ensure that our methodology remains aligned with both the data and the goals of the project.  After the Figma session, we participated in a virtual escape room activity, which was both fun and challenging. It required teamwork, communication, and creative thinking to solve the puzzles. We ended the day with a lab session alongside our project team and graduate mentor. During this meeting, our mentor introduced us to a research paper related to our project, and we spent time reading and discussing its key ideas to better understand the foundation of the work we will be doing this summer.
  Throughout the day, we continued refining our understanding of the overall direction of our research. By combining insights from our literature review work with our ongoing dataset exploration, we were able to better connect the theoretical foundations of our project with the practical steps we are taking to implement it. Overall, today's activities helped me improve both my research communication skills and my understanding of how our dataset will support the work we plan to do.

reflection: |
  Today’s sessions reminded me that successful research depends not only on technical skills but also on the ability to communicate ideas effectively. Learning about transitional words and phrases showed me how small details in writing can significantly improve the clarity and flow of a research paper. As our project continues to take shape, I am beginning to see how the different pieces of the research process fit together, from literature reviews and writing to data analysis and methodology development. Overall, I left the day with a greater appreciation for both the communication and technical aspects of research, and I feel more confident in our project's direction moving forward.
