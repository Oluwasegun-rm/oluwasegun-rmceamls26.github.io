what_i_learned: |
  Today I continued making progress on our research paper by beginning the Results section. Building on the methodology that I worked on yesterday, I started drafting how we will present the findings from our experiments in a clear and organized manner. Rather than simply listing model performance metrics, I spent time thinking about how to structure the section so that it effectively communicates the story behind our results and supports the contributions of our research.

  As part of this process, I reviewed the experimental outputs from our baseline models and fusion approaches to determine which results are most important to include. I considered how different evaluation metrics, comparisons between fusion strategies, and observations related to modality dominance can best demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework. This helped me understand that the Results section is not only about reporting numbers but also about presenting evidence that supports the research questions and hypotheses explored throughout the project.

  I also revisited related research papers to observe how other authors present experimental findings through tables, figures, and written analysis. Studying these examples gave me a better understanding of how to balance technical detail with readability while ensuring that the results are presented in a logical and meaningful sequence. Overall, today's work helped me develop a stronger appreciation for how careful organization and thoughtful interpretation contribute to a well-written research paper.
