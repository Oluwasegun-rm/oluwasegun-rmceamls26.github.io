what_i_learned: |
  Today focused on evaluating state-of-the-art multimodal learning approaches that will serve as comparison models for our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework. I spent the day running and understanding both MOGONET and Similarity Network Fusion (SNF), two established methods for integrating multiple omics datasets. Implementing these models helped me better understand how existing multimodal approaches perform and provided important baselines for evaluating the effectiveness of our own framework.

  As I worked through these models, I gained a better understanding of the different strategies they use to combine information from genotype, gene expression, and DNA methylation data. Rather than simply measuring predictive performance, I also examined how each approach handles the interaction between modalities and how they compare to the adaptive signal-gating mechanism that we are proposing in SGOF.

  Throughout the day, I continued discussing implementation details and evaluation strategies with my graduate mentor. These conversations helped clarify how we will perform fair comparisons between SGOF, MOGONET, and SNF using consistent preprocessing, cross-validation, and evaluation metrics. Establishing these comparison models is an important step because it allows us to objectively determine whether our proposed framework offers meaningful improvements over existing methods.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. Most of the work involved understanding the implementation details of existing multimodal models and ensuring they were configured consistently for fair comparison with our proposed framework.

reflection: |
  Today reinforced the importance of comparing new ideas against strong existing methods. Rather than assuming our proposed approach will perform better, we are building a rigorous evaluation process to determine where SGOF provides improvements and where further refinement may be needed. I am beginning to appreciate how much of research is centered on designing fair experiments that produce reliable and meaningful conclusions.
