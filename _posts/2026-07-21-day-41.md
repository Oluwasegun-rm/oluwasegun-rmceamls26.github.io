what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on completing two of the final major sections of our research paper: the Results and Discussion sections. A significant portion of the day was spent refining and finalizing the Results section by reviewing our experimental findings, ensuring that the tables accurately represented our model performance, and confirming that the written explanations clearly communicated the key observations from our evaluation. We worked to make sure the results flowed logically and highlighted the performance differences between the baseline methods, existing multimodal approaches, and our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework.

  I also began writing the Discussion section of the paper. This involved interpreting our experimental findings rather than simply reporting them. We discussed why our proposed framework performed the way it did, what the results imply about modality dominance in multi-omics learning, and how our findings compare with existing approaches in the literature. Writing this section required me to think beyond the numbers and consider the broader significance of our work, its strengths, and the limitations that future research could address.

  Throughout the day, I collaborated closely with my teammates to review different sections of the paper, refine our wording, and ensure that our interpretations were supported by the experimental evidence. This process reinforced the importance of connecting technical results back to the original research questions and clearly explaining why our contributions are meaningful.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The primary challenge was ensuring that our discussion accurately reflected the experimental results while avoiding overinterpreting the findings and keeping our conclusions well supported by the data.

reflection: |
  Today's work showed me that the Discussion section is one of the most important parts of a research paper because it explains the meaning behind the results rather than simply presenting them. Writing this section encouraged me to think critically about our contributions, how they fit within existing research, and what future work could build upon our findings. It was rewarding to see the project coming together as we finalized major sections of the paper, and I feel much more confident in both my technical understanding and my scientific writing skills than I did at the beginning of the program.
