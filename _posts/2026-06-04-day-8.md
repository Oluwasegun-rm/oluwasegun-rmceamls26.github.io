what_i_learned: |
  Today began with another insightful session on the importance of literature reviews in the research process. We discussed how reviewing existing research helps researchers understand the current state of knowledge within a field and identify gaps where new contributions can be made. A major focus of the session was learning how to identify research gaps and use them to justify the purpose and significance of a research project. We also practiced writing background statements, which helped me better understand how researchers introduce a problem, provide context, and explain why a particular area of study is important.

  Later in the day, we met with our project teams during our research lab sessions. During this time, we continued developing our project design document, focusing specifically on the methodology, validation, and implementation sections. Through discussions with my teammates and graduate mentor, I gained a better understanding of how research plans are structured and how important it is to clearly define the methods that will be used to answer research questions. We also discussed how we will evaluate the effectiveness of our approach and ensure that our results are meaningful and reliable. Overall, today's activities gave me a deeper appreciation for the planning and preparation that take place before the actual implementation phase of a research project.

reflection: |
  Today's activities reinforced the importance of having a strong foundation before beginning any research project. Learning more about literature reviews and research gaps helped me understand that successful research is not just about finding answers, but also about identifying meaningful questions that have not yet been fully explored. Practicing background statements also helped me improve my ability to communicate the significance of a research problem in a clear and organized way.

  Working on our design document with my team gave me a greater appreciation for the amount of thought that goes into planning a research project. Discussing our methodology, validation strategies, and implementation plans helped me see how each component contributes to producing reliable and credible results. I also enjoyed collaborating with my teammates and hearing different perspectives as we worked toward a common goal. Overall, I left the day with a stronger understanding of the research process and greater confidence in the direction of our project.
