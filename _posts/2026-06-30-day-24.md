what_i_learned: |
  Today our research cohort traveled to Washington, D.C. to attend the AWS Summit, where we had the opportunity to learn from industry professionals through keynote presentations, technical workshops, and networking sessions. It was exciting to experience a large technology conference and see how artificial intelligence, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and software engineering are being applied across different industries.

  During the summit, I attended workshops focused on building AI-powered solutions on AWS and developing scalable LLMOps workflows for deploying and managing large language models. These sessions introduced practical concepts related to generative AI, cloud infrastructure, AI deployment pipelines, and the growing role of AI in modern software development. The workshops helped me better understand how machine learning models move beyond experimentation into real-world production environments.

  Beyond the technical sessions, I had the opportunity to meet professionals from several companies, including Booz Allen Hamilton, Datadog, and AWS. Speaking with engineers and other professionals gave me valuable insight into their day-to-day work, the technologies they use, and the skills that are most valuable in today's technology industry. It was encouraging to hear about the different career paths available and learn how AI is becoming integrated into many areas of software engineering and cloud computing.

  Overall, attending the AWS Summit broadened my perspective on both the technical and professional sides of the technology industry. It was a great opportunity to connect what I have been learning in research with how these technologies are being applied at scale in industry.
