what_i_learned: |
  Today I focused on preparing for the next major implementation phase of our research project as our team begins transitioning toward building our graph neural network. A large part of the day was spent understanding modality dominance, one of the key challenges in multimodal learning. Since our project integrates genotype, gene expression, DNA methylation, and other biological data sources, it is important that one modality does not overpower the others during training. If this occurs, the model may rely too heavily on one source of information while failing to learn meaningful patterns from the remaining modalities.
  To better understand this challenge, I spent time reviewing related research papers and examining how other researchers have addressed modality dominance in multimodal machine learning. I explored different approaches used to balance the contribution of multiple data modalities and learned why preserving information from each biological layer is essential for building a robust graph neural network. These papers also helped me connect many of the concepts we have been discussing over the past few weeks to the implementation work that lies ahead.
  Throughout the day, I also met with my teammates and graduate mentor to discuss how these ideas fit into our project's methodology and how we plan to approach this next stage of development. Since I will begin implementing this portion of the project tomorrow, today's discussions and literature review gave me a much stronger understanding of both the underlying theory and the practical considerations involved. Overall, I feel much better prepared to contribute to one of the most important components of our research project.
