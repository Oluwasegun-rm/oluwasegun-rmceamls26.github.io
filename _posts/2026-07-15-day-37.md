what_i_learned: |
  Today I continued building on the implementation and evaluation work from yesterday by further analyzing the different fusion approaches used in our project. A significant portion of the day was spent running additional experiments, comparing model performance, and validating the results across the various fusion families we are evaluating. This allowed me to better understand how each approach integrates information from the different omics modalities and how those design choices affect predictive performance.

  I also continued refining the implementation of our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework. Rather than simply comparing overall model performance, I focused on understanding how each modality contributed to the final predictions and whether the adaptive weighting mechanism behaved as expected. Interpreting these results helped me connect the experimental findings back to our original hypothesis that not all biological modalities contribute equally and that accounting for modality dominance can lead to more robust multimodal learning.

  In addition to the technical implementation, our team continued discussing the interpretation of our experimental results and how they should be communicated in our research paper. We worked to ensure that the conclusions we draw are supported by the evidence produced through our experiments and that the presentation of our results accurately reflects the behavior of the different models we evaluated. These discussions reinforced the importance of combining rigorous experimentation with clear scientific communication.
