what_i_learned: |
  Today was a very informative and engaging day. One of the main topics we explored was Gradio, a Python library that makes it easy to build simple web applications and user interfaces for machine learning and AI projects. Through hands-on examples, I learned how Gradio can be used to quickly create interactive applications that allow users to interact with models and other Python-based tools without requiring extensive frontend development knowledge. It was interesting to see how quickly a functional interface could be created using just a few lines of code.

  We also spent time learning more about the research project that I will be working on throughout the summer. Our discussions with the project team and graduate mentor helped me develop a clearer understanding of the project's goals, the problems we are trying to solve, and the technologies that will be involved. This gave me a better sense of the direction our work will take over the coming weeks.

  Another highlight of the day was participating in an interactive game developed by Google Research. The activity walked us through different stages of the research process and demonstrated how AI and machine learning can be used to support and enhance human decision-making rather than simply replace people. Through the game, I learned about the importance of collecting diverse datasets, cleaning and preparing data before training, sourcing data from multiple locations, and avoiding biases that can negatively affect model performance. The activity also emphasized the importance of testing and evaluating models to ensure they perform reliably and fairly. Overall, the session provided valuable insight into both the technical and ethical considerations involved in developing AI systems.
