what_i_learned: |
  Today’s sessions focused on Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs), and I learned a lot about how modern AI systems work behind the scenes. We started by discussing what LLMs are and why they are called "large language models." We learned that they are trained on massive amounts of text data and use deep learning techniques to recognize patterns in language, allowing them to generate human-like responses. We also explored how these models are trained and the role that tokens play in helping AI process and understand text. Another important concept we covered was embeddings, which are numerical representations of words and pieces of text that allow AI models to understand relationships and meaning between different concepts.

  In addition, we discussed the differences between traditional AI approaches and deep learning. It was interesting to see how traditional AI often relies on explicit rules and human-defined logic, while deep learning systems learn patterns directly from large datasets. Understanding this distinction helped me better appreciate why modern AI systems have become so powerful and capable of performing complex tasks.

  Later in the session, we participated in an interactive activity where we were shown various images and had to determine whether they were AI-generated or created by humans. This activity highlighted how advanced image generation technology has become and how difficult it can sometimes be to distinguish between real and AI-generated content. Overall, the session provided valuable insight into the foundations of modern AI and helped me better understand many of the concepts that are frequently discussed in the field today.
