what_i_learned: |
  Today was primarily focused on writing the methodology section of our research paper. This involved carefully documenting the technical workflow that my team has developed over the course of the project and presenting it in a logical, sequential manner. Rather than simply describing the steps we performed, I learned the importance of explaining the reasoning behind each design decision, the order in which the methods are applied, and how each stage contributes to the overall research objective.

  As I worked on the methodology, I revisited many of the technical components of our project, including data preprocessing, patient alignment across multiple omics datasets, baseline model development, cross-validation, multimodal fusion strategies, and our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework. Writing these sections required me to think carefully about how each component connects to the next so that someone reading the paper could understand and reproduce our implementation.

  I also spent time discussing the methodology with my teammates to ensure that our implementation details were accurately represented and that the paper reflected the work we have completed throughout the project. Translating technical implementation into clear academic writing helped me appreciate that a methodology section is more than a list of steps—it is a detailed explanation of how the research was designed, implemented, and evaluated.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The primary challenge was determining how much technical detail to include while keeping the methodology organized, concise, and easy for readers to follow.

reflection: |
  Today's work showed me that writing a research paper requires a deep understanding of the project itself. As I documented our methodology, I found myself reflecting on many of the technical decisions we have made throughout the summer and why each one was important. It was rewarding to see how the different pieces of our research—from data preprocessing and model development to evaluation and multimodal fusion—fit together into a single, cohesive workflow. I also gained a greater appreciation for the importance of writing in a way that allows other researchers to understand and reproduce our work.
