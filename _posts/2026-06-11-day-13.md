what_i_learned: |
  Today's work focused on one of the most important foundations of any research project: finding and evaluating high-quality datasets. Our team spent a significant amount of time searching for datasets that align with the goals of our project and exploring different sources from which reliable data can be obtained. As part of this process, we examined datasets from government agencies, research institutions, and other trusted sources to determine which ones would be most appropriate for our work.

  One of the most valuable lessons I learned today came from discussions with my graduate mentor about the importance of using verifiable and trustworthy data sources. Since we are conducting research, the quality of the data directly impacts the quality and validity of the conclusions we can draw. We discussed how peer-reviewed datasets and datasets from reputable organizations provide a stronger foundation for research because they have often undergone extensive validation and quality checks. This helped me better understand that data selection is not simply about finding information, but about ensuring that the information is credible, reliable, and relevant to the research question being investigated.

  Throughout the day, we also had productive project discussions with both our professor and graduate mentor. These conversations helped us think critically about our project's direction, the types of data we need, and how our methodology should align with the resources available to us.

  In addition, I spent time learning how to use AI tools more effectively to support the research process. I learned how AI can assist with reading and summarizing research papers, explaining technical concepts, breaking down academic jargon, and helping generate better questions during the research process. These tools are becoming increasingly valuable as I continue to navigate unfamiliar topics and build my understanding of the field.

reflection: |
  Today helped me realize how important curiosity and resourcefulness are in research. In the past, when I encountered a technical concept or piece of academic terminology that I did not understand, I would sometimes feel overwhelmed by how much there was to learn. However, I am gradually becoming more comfortable with the idea that I do not need to know everything immediately. Instead, I can use the tools and resources available to me to learn, investigate, and build my understanding over time.

  Learning how to effectively use AI tools has been particularly helpful in this regard. Whether I am reading a research paper, encountering unfamiliar terminology, or trying to understand a complex concept, I now have tools that can help me break information down into more manageable pieces. Rather than being discouraged by what I do not know, I find myself becoming more curious and motivated to explore it further.

  I also gained a greater appreciation for the importance of data quality in research. The discussions about sourcing reliable datasets reinforced the idea that strong research begins with strong data.   Overall, today strengthened both my research skills and my confidence as a learner. I left the day feeling more prepared to ask questions, seek answers, and continue growing throughout this research experience.
