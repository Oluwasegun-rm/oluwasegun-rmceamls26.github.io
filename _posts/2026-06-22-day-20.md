what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on the first phase of implementing our research methodology. A significant portion of the day was spent working with our datasets and preparing them for future analysis and modeling. This involved loading data from multiple sources, performing initial data cleaning, and examining the structure of the datasets to ensure they were suitable for use in our project.

  As part of the preprocessing process, I worked on organizing and standardizing the data. This included transposing data where necessary, correcting row and column structures, and ensuring that feature mappings were consistent across different datasets. Since our project relies on integrating information from multiple sources, it is important that variables, labels, and data structures align correctly. I learned that even small inconsistencies in naming conventions or data organization can create significant challenges later in the modeling process.

  We also began implementing the initial stages of our machine learning pipeline. Specifically, I worked on developing a basic linear regression pipeline that will serve as a baseline model for our project. Establishing a baseline is an important step because it provides a reference point that can be used to evaluate whether more advanced models actually improve performance. This helped me better understand how machine learning projects are often built incrementally, beginning with simpler approaches before moving on to more complex methods.

  Earlier in the day, we participated in an interactive group activity that gave everyone a chance to take a break from research and engage with one another. The activity was both fun and collaborative, and it provided a welcome opportunity to relax, connect with other participants, and recharge before returning to our project work.

reflection: |
  Today's work helped me better understand the gap between designing a machine learning methodology and actually implementing it in practice. Many of the concepts we discussed during project planning, such as data alignment, preprocessing, cross-validation, and evaluation metrics, became much more meaningful once I had to apply them to a real dataset.

  I also gained a greater appreciation for the importance of establishing strong baseline models. Although the logistic regression models did not achieve particularly strong performance, the results provide a valuable reference point that will help us evaluate whether future, more sophisticated approaches are actually improving predictive performance. Overall, today's activities strengthened both my technical understanding of machine learning workflows and my appreciation for the careful attention to detail required when working with real-world biomedical data.
