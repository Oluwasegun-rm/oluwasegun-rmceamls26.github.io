what_i_learned: |
  Today’s sessions focused on computer vision and its applications in artificial intelligence. We learned what computer vision is and how it enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from images and videos. One of the key concepts we discussed was the difference between image classification and object detection. While classification focuses on identifying what is present in an image, object detection goes a step further by locating and identifying multiple objects within the image using bounding boxes.

  We also explored object tracking and how computer vision systems can follow objects as they move across video frames. This helped me understand how technologies such as surveillance systems, autonomous vehicles, and sports analytics are able to continuously monitor objects in real time. Another important concept we learned about was bounding boxes and how they are used to identify the location of detected objects within an image.

  To reinforce these concepts, we worked hands-on with YOLO (You Only Look Once) and OpenCV in Google Colab. We experimented with images and tested object detection models to see how they identified and labeled objects. It was exciting to apply the concepts we had learned and observe the results directly. Later in the day, we participated in our weekly showcase presentations where each research group presented their progress and discussed what they had accomplished throughout the week. Listening to the presentations gave me insight into the diverse range of projects being conducted within the program and the different approaches teams are taking to solve their research problems.
