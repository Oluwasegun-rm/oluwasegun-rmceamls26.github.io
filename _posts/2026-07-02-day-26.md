what_i_learned: |
  Today was centered around preparing for the paper writing phase of our research project while continuing to build my understanding of the technical concepts behind our implementation. I spent much of the day conducting literature reviews on research papers that closely relate to our project. Rather than focusing solely on identifying research gaps, I paid closer attention to how the papers were written, how different sections were organized, the language used to present ideas, and how figures and diagrams were designed to communicate results effectively. This gave me a better understanding of what makes a well-written research paper and how technical work is presented in a clear and structured manner.

  In addition to studying paper structure, I continued exploring concepts that are directly relevant to our project. I spent time learning about modality dominance and how it can negatively affect multimodal learning models when one data modality contributes significantly more information than the others. Since our project combines multiple biological data types, understanding this challenge is important as we move toward implementing our graph neural network.

  I also read about the concept of uninformative modalities and how they can impact fusion models. I learned that when one modality contains little useful information or introduces excessive noise, it can reduce the effectiveness of the entire model if it is not handled appropriately. Understanding these concepts helped me better appreciate the importance of designing multimodal AI systems that effectively balance information from each data source instead of treating every modality equally. Overall, today's work strengthened both my understanding of research communication and the theoretical foundations that will support the next stage of our project.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The primary challenge was understanding several new concepts related to multimodal learning, but reading multiple research papers and discussing them with my team helped clarify many of the ideas.

reflection: |
  Today's work reminded me that conducting research involves much more than implementing algorithms. Learning how experienced researchers organize their papers, communicate technical ideas, and present their results is just as valuable as understanding the underlying methods. At the same time, exploring concepts such as modality dominance and uninformative modalities gave me a deeper appreciation for the challenges involved in building robust multimodal AI systems. I feel better prepared for both the implementation and writing phases of our project as we continue making progress.
