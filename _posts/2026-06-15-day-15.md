what_i_learned: |
  Today marked the in-person kickoff of the summer research program and provided a great introduction to the experiences and opportunities that lie ahead. We began with an orientation session where we learned more about the structure of the program, the expectations for participants, and the various activities that will take place throughout the coming weeks. This session helped me better understand how the program is organized and what I can expect as I continue my research journey this summer.

  One of the most interesting sessions of the day focused on AI and Ethics. During this presentation, we discussed the importance of responsible AI development and the challenges that can arise when building AI systems. We learned about bias in AI and how the quality and diversity of training data can significantly impact model outputs. The session highlighted how decisions made during data collection, preprocessing, and model development can influence fairness, accuracy, and reliability. It was insightful to see how ethical considerations are just as important as technical performance when developing AI technologies.

  Later in the day, we participated in a workshop on Data Analysis using Python. The workshop provided an overview of a basic data analysis and machine learning workflow, beginning with loading and exploring datasets before progressing through preprocessing, analysis, and model development stages. Through hands-on examples, I gained a better understanding of how data moves through a typical machine learning pipeline and how Python can be used to support each step of the process. Overall, today's activities provided a strong foundation for the technical and research skills that will be developed throughout the program.
blockers: |
  One challenge today was absorbing a large amount of new information in a short period of time. The orientation, ethics discussion, and technical workshop introduced many concepts and ideas, making it important to review and reflect on the material to fully understand how everything connects.

reflection: |
  Today's kickoff activities reinforced the idea that successful AI research requires both technical expertise and thoughtful decision-making. The AI and Ethics session was particularly impactful because it emphasized that building effective models is not enough; researchers must also consider fairness, bias, and the broader impact their work may have on society. It made me think more critically about the responsibility that comes with developing AI systems.

  I also enjoyed the Data Analysis workshop because it connected many of the concepts I have previously encountered into a more complete workflow. Seeing how data is loaded, explored, prepared, and eventually used for machine learning helped me better understand the bigger picture of the data science process. Overall, today was an exciting start to the program, and I left feeling motivated to continue learning, collaborating with others, and developing both my technical and research skills throughout the summer.
