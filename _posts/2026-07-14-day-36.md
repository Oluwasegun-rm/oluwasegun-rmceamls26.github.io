what_i_learned: |
  Today was divided between improving our research paper and continuing the technical evaluation of our proposed model. My team and I spent a significant amount of time refining the abstract, introduction, and background sections of our paper. We carefully revised the wording to ensure that our ideas were communicated clearly, logically, and in a style that aligns with academic research writing. This process involved improving sentence structure, strengthening transitions between ideas, and making sure the motivation and contributions of our work were presented effectively.

  On the technical side, I continued running comparisons across the different fusion approaches that we are evaluating for our project. I analyzed the performance of the various fusion families by comparing their predictive scores across the different omics modalities. These experiments helped me better understand how each fusion strategy behaves and provided additional evidence for evaluating the strengths and weaknesses of our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework.

  Throughout the day, I discussed both the writing and technical aspects of the project with my teammates and graduate mentor. Balancing research writing with model evaluation helped me appreciate that producing quality research requires not only developing strong methods but also communicating those methods clearly and supporting them with thorough experimental results.
