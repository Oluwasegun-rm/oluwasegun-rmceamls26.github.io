what_i_learned: |
  Today was dedicated to preparing for the final research symposium by developing and refining our team's presentation slides. We spent time organizing the content to clearly communicate our project's motivation, methodology, experimental results, and key contributions. Rather than simply transferring information from our paper to the slides, we focused on presenting our research as a clear and engaging story that would be easy for a broad audience to follow.

  A significant portion of the day involved deciding how best to present our findings through figures, tables, and concise explanations. We carefully selected the most important experimental results and discussed how to highlight the performance of our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework while providing meaningful comparisons with the baseline and existing multimodal models. This process helped me understand that an effective presentation is not about including every detail, but about emphasizing the most impactful findings and communicating them clearly.

  Throughout the day, I collaborated closely with my teammates to improve the flow of the presentation and refine how each section connects to the next. We practiced explaining our work in a way that balances technical accuracy with accessibility, ensuring that both technical and non-technical audience members will be able to understand the significance of our research. Overall, today's work strengthened my presentation skills and reinforced the importance of effectively communicating scientific research.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The primary challenge was deciding which technical details to include on the slides while keeping the presentation concise, engaging, and easy for the audience to follow.

reflection: |
  Today's work reminded me that presenting research is very different from writing a research paper. A presentation requires distilling weeks of work into a clear narrative that communicates the motivation, methodology, results, and impact in a short amount of time. Collaborating with my teammates to refine our slides helped me appreciate the importance of storytelling in scientific communication, and I feel more prepared for the final symposium presentation this weekend.
