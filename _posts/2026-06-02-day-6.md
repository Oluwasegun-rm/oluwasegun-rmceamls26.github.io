what_i_learned: |
  Today's activities focused heavily on developing research skills and gaining a deeper understanding of the research process. One of the most valuable lessons I learned was how to effectively read and analyze research abstracts. We discussed the key components that make up a strong abstract, including the background of the study, the research gap being addressed, the hypothesis or research objective, the methods used, and the major findings. Learning how to quickly identify these elements will help me better understand research papers and determine their relevance to my own work.

We also explored the importance of conducting a thorough literature review. I learned that literature reviews are not simply summaries of previous work, but rather a critical step in understanding the current state of knowledge within a field. By reviewing related research, it becomes easier to identify gaps in existing studies, understand the methods other researchers have used, and determine where new contributions can be made. This process helps ensure that research efforts are both meaningful and well-informed.

In addition, I spent time gaining a deeper understanding of the problem statement for my project and reading papers related to the topic. Reviewing these materials helped me better understand the goals of the project, the challenges researchers are trying to solve, and how our work may contribute to the broader research area.

We also continued working with Gradio, a Python library used to create interactive web applications and user interfaces. Through this session, I learned more about how Gradio can be used to quickly build user-friendly interfaces for AI and machine learning applications, making it easier for users to interact with models and other tools.
