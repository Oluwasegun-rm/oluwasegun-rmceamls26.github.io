what_i_learned: |
  Today I focused on implementing and evaluating the first baseline models for our research project using the real multi-omics dataset. I began by loading and aligning the different biological data modalities, including genotype, gene expression, and DNA methylation data, so that each patient's information was correctly matched across all datasets. After aligning the data, I transformed each modality into patient-by-feature matrices that could be used directly for machine learning and generated the corresponding responder and non-responder labels for prediction.

  Once the data was prepared, I trained baseline machine learning models on each omics modality independently using proper within-fold preprocessing and stratified cross-validation. This workflow ensured that preprocessing steps were performed correctly during each fold, helping to prevent data leakage and produce more reliable performance estimates. Implementing this pipeline helped me gain a better understanding of how evaluation procedures influence the validity of machine learning experiments.

  The baseline results were particularly interesting because they revealed clear differences between the biological modalities. The genotype data produced relatively poor predictive performance, while the gene expression and DNA methylation datasets showed stronger predictive signals. These findings align with the motivation behind our project, which suggests that some modalities contribute much more useful information than others. This observation further reinforces why our proposed Signal Gated Omic Fusion (SGOF) framework is important, as it aims to prevent less informative modalities from dominating or degrading the performance of multimodal models.

  In addition to the implementation work, I had several discussions with my graduate mentor about interpreting the baseline results and how they will inform the next phase of our project. These conversations helped connect the experimental findings to the broader research questions we are investigating and prepared us for the transition toward implementing the multimodal fusion model.

blockers: |
  No major blockers today. The primary focus was verifying that the preprocessing and evaluation pipeline was implemented correctly so that the baseline results could serve as a reliable benchmark for future experiments.

reflection: |
  Today's work was exciting because it represented one of the first opportunities to evaluate our project using real experimental results rather than planning or preprocessing alone. Seeing measurable differences in predictive performance across the different omics modalities helped me better understand the motivation behind our proposed framework and confirmed why handling modality dominance is an important research problem. It was rewarding to see the theoretical concepts we have been studying begin to appear in the actual data, and I am looking forward to comparing these baseline models with our proposed SGOF approach in the coming weeks.
