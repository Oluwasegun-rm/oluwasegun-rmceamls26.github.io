what_i_learned: |

  Today was dedicated to preparing for the upcoming Mid-Summer Research Symposium through a series of mock presentations. My team and I spent time refining our presentation slides, organizing the flow of our content, and practicing how we would communicate our research to an audience. We reviewed each section of the presentation to ensure that our project's motivation, methodology, progress, and future work were presented in a logical and easy-to-follow manner.

  During the mock presentations, we practiced our individual speaking parts and received constructive feedback from our peers, graduate mentors, and faculty members. The feedback helped us identify areas where our explanations could be made clearer, where additional context was needed, and where we could improve the overall delivery of our presentation. It also reinforced the importance of communicating technical concepts in a way that is understandable to people who may not have the same research background.

  Throughout the day, I also listened to other research groups present their projects and observed how they organized their presentations, explained their methodologies, and responded to questions. Seeing different presentation styles and learning from the feedback given to other teams provided useful ideas that I can apply when presenting my own work. Overall, today's activities helped me improve both my presentation skills and my confidence in communicating our research effectively.

reflection: |

  Today's mock presentations reminded me that conducting good research is only part of the process—being able to communicate that research effectively is just as important. Preparing and practicing our presentation helped me identify areas where I needed a stronger understanding of our project so that I could explain it more confidently and naturally.

  I also benefited from watching other teams present and listening to the feedback they received. It gave me new ideas for improving our own presentation and reinforced the importance of clear communication, confidence, and preparation. Overall, I feel more prepared for the Mid-Summer Research Symposium and look forward to presenting the progress our team has made.
