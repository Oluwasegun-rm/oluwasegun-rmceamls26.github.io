what_i_learned: |
  Today's sessions focused on the evolving role of artificial intelligence and how it is changing the way software is developed. One of the key topics we discussed was context engineering and the importance of effectively working with AI systems to improve productivity and problem-solving. Rather than viewing software engineering as solely writing code, we explored how modern developers are increasingly becoming orchestrators who are able to break complex tasks into smaller components, provide the necessary context, and leverage AI systems to assist with different stages of development.

  We also learned about agent-based workflows and how AI tools can be assigned specialized tasks while working together toward a larger objective. This included discussions about providing AI systems with the right tools, instructions, context, and constraints to achieve better results. The session highlighted how understanding how to work with AI may become just as important as traditional programming skills in many future software engineering workflows.

  In addition, we spent time learning more about Large Language Models (LLMs) and how they operate. We discussed concepts related to model training, reasoning, context, and how these systems process information to generate responses. These discussions helped me gain a better understanding of the technologies that power many of today's AI tools.

  Later in the day, we participated in our weekly showcase and project discussions. I had the opportunity to share updates on my own project's progress while also listening to presentations from other teams. It was interesting to hear about the different challenges, accomplishments, and research directions being explored throughout the program. Learning about the experiences of other participants provided valuable insight into the variety of ways AI and research are being applied across different projects.
